# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

HiveMind Protocol is built on the idea that multiple perspectives produce better outcomes — that applies to our AI agents and to our community. We're committed to making this project a welcoming, respectful space for everyone, regardless of experience level, background, identity, or perspective.

This code of conduct applies to all project spaces: GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, Discord/chat channels, and any other forums where you represent or interact with the HiveMind community.

---

## What Respectful Collaboration Looks Like

We expect everyone participating in this project to:

- **Be welcoming and inclusive.** Use language that invites participation. Remember that what's obvious to you might be brand new to someone else.
- **Give and receive feedback gracefully.** Code reviews and design discussions should focus on the work, not the person. "This approach might cause X problem because Y" is constructive. "This is wrong" without explanation is not.
- **Assume good intent, but address impact.** If something someone said or did had a negative effect, it's okay to raise it — even if they didn't mean harm.
- **Credit others' work.** If you build on someone's idea, say so. If someone helped you debug something, acknowledge it.
- **Ask questions without apology.** There are no stupid questions. If something in the codebase is confusing, that's feedback on the documentation, not on you.
- **Respect people's time.** Keep issues focused, PRs scoped, and discussions on-topic. If a conversation is going in circles, suggest taking it to a dedicated thread or call.

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following will not be tolerated in any project space:

- **Harassment or intimidation.** This includes unwelcome comments about someone's identity, experience level, or personal characteristics; sustained disruption of discussions; and any form of bullying or threatening language.
- **Sexualized language or imagery.** This includes sexual comments, jokes, or imagery in any project space — issues, PRs, chat, documentation, or code comments.
- **Personal attacks.** Disagreeing with a technical decision is fine. Attacking the person who made it is not. "That design choice is flawed because X" is okay. "You clearly don't understand Y" is not.
- **Trolling or bad-faith participation.** Deliberately derailing conversations, posting inflammatory content, or engaging solely to provoke a reaction.
- **Doxxing.** Publishing someone's private information (real name, address, email, wallet address) without their explicit consent.
- **Sustained disruption.** Repeatedly posting off-topic content, spamming issues or PRs, or deliberately slowing down project progress.
- **Retaliation.** Taking adverse action against someone for reporting a code of conduct violation or participating in an investigation.

---

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to:

- All GitHub repositories under the HiveMind organization
- Issue trackers, pull requests, and code review comments
- Project chat channels (Discord, Telegram, or similar)
- Community events, meetups, or hackathons where you're representing the project
- Any public space where you're acting as a representative of the project

It does **not** apply to your personal social media accounts or activities unrelated to HiveMind, unless those activities directly target another community member in connection with their participation in this project.

---

## Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this code of conduct:

1. **Email the maintainers** at **hivemind-conduct@proton.me** (or the contact listed in the repository's SECURITY.md if one exists). Include:
   - What happened (specific quotes or screenshots if available)
   - Where it happened (link to the issue, PR, or chat message)
   - When it happened
   - Any other context that might help

2. **You can also report directly on GitHub** by flagging the specific comment or issue using GitHub's built-in reporting tools.

3. **All reports are confidential.** The maintainers will not share your identity with the person being reported unless you explicitly consent. If a maintainer has a conflict of interest (e.g., they're involved in the incident), they will recuse themselves.

You will receive an acknowledgment within **48 hours** confirming that your report was received and is being reviewed.

---

## Enforcement

When a report is received, the maintainers will:

1. **Acknowledge receipt** within 48 hours.
2. **Review the report** by examining the relevant conversations, context, and any evidence provided. The maintainers may reach out to the reporter, the person being reported, or witnesses for clarification.
3. **Decide on a response** based on the severity and context of the violation. Possible outcomes include:

| Response | When It's Used |
|---|---|
| **Private conversation** | First-time, minor violations — a direct message explaining what was inappropriate and why, with a request to stop. |
| **Public warning** | Repeated minor violations, or a single violation that had visible impact on other community members. A comment on the relevant thread noting the behavior and this code of conduct. |
| **Temporary ban** | Serious violations or a pattern of behavior that makes others feel unwelcome. The person is blocked from all project spaces for a defined period (typically 7–30 days). |
| **Permanent ban** | Severe violations (e.g., harassment, threats, doxxing), or continued violations after a temporary ban. The person is permanently removed from all project spaces. |

4. **Communicate the outcome** to the reporter. If the outcome involves a public action (warning or ban), a brief, factual note will be posted in the relevant space without revealing the reporter's identity.

---

## Appealing a Decision

If you believe an enforcement action against you was unjust or disproportionate, you may appeal by emailing **hivemind-conduct@proton.me** with:

- The action you're appealing
- Why you believe it was incorrect or disproportionate
- Any additional context

Appeals are reviewed by a different maintainer than the one who made the original decision, when possible. You'll receive a response within **7 days**.
